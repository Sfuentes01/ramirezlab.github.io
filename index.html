Ramírez Lab

Computational Biophysics, Bioinformatics & Drug Design

Welcome to the Ramírez Lab

We are a multidisciplinary group focusing on the study of biomolecular systems by using both, computational and experimental approaches. We aim to use computational methods - broadening the way to describe molecular processes - to combine and use them together with wet lab analyses for drug design and development procedures. Our work involves collaboration with medicinal chemists, biochemists, and biologists, and we are part of the Institute of Biomedical Sciences at Universidad Autónoma de Chile.

Active research areas:

  • Structural insights into the substrate specificity and transport mechanisms in the Nitrate/Peptide Transporter (NPF) Family

  • Targeting cancer polypharmacology

  • Promiscuous-drug design of molecules targeting neurodegenerative diseases

  • Ion occupancy in potassium channels

  • Unraveling the role of the side-fenestration in ion channels gating

  • Understanding the binding mechanisms of endo and phytocannabinoids into the human CB1 receptor
